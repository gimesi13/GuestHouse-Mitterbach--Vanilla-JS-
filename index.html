Covid rules during your stay in Austria

Arrivals to Austria currently do NOT need "3-G" proof (vaccinated/recovered or tested). During your stay, the following rules apply: FFP 2 masks are no longer mandatory in essential shops and when using public transport in all Austrian federal provinces except for Vienna.

In Vienna only, FFP 2 masks remain compulsory on public transport and in pharmacies. Children under the age of 6 are exempt, those from 6 to 13 can wear a regular mask while FFP2 masks are mandatory from the age of 14.

Hello, It's Dori and Geri and we are the heart and soul of Guesthouse Mitterbach :) . When we found this little piece of paradise hidden away in the alps, we fell in love with it immediately. We hope to share this feeling with you and that you will enjoy your time here as much as we do.

Skiing and Snowboadring is our passion and we LOVE animals! Feel free to bring your belowed pets with you on your holiday to Mitterbach, we will make sure that they are happy and taken care of even while you are on the slopes or enjoy other activities that might not be suitable for your furry companion.

-SLOPES-

Gemeindealpe:


In addition to the long forest blue slopes of the Gemeindealpe mountain, which is home to our guest house, it also has red tracks of varying difficulty, as well as one of the steepest black tracks in Lower Austria. There is a practice track at the foot of the mountain for beginners, and a Snowpark has been built higher up. The tracks have recently been fitted with plenty of new snow cannons so they are always snow safe. There is also a 12-kilometer cross-country ski run at the foot of the mountain.

Click here for more information

Mariazeller-Bürgeralpe:


It can be reached by car or bus in 10 minutes. This small town is a famous attraction among visitors in winter. In addition to its rich Christian culture and charming Advent fair, it is also a popular destination for its ski slopes. These tracks are mainly recommended for beginners, but there are also advanced tracks.

Click here for more information

Annaberg:


This mountain is a bit further away, about half an hour by bus, but it is also worth visiting here, because there are exciting slopes here as well, and the ski pass purchased from us is also valid here.
